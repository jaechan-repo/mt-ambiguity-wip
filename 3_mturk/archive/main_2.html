Usage of idioms: Literal, Figurative, or Ambiguous?

Thank you for participating in this HIT.

Please read the instructions below completely before proceeding with the task.

Your task is to identify the usage of a given idiom in different sentences. The idiom and its dictionary definition will be provided in the following format:

[Idiom] "(idiom goes here)"
[Dictionary Definition] (dictionary meaning of the idiom goes here)


You will be given three different sentences that include this idiom. For each of the sentence, choose among the four choices demonstrated below.

If the idiom is used in its idiomatic meaning within the sentence (i.e., used figuratively rather than literally), then select idiomatic. In case you are unfamiliar with the figurative use of the idiom, use the provided [Dictionary Definition] as a reference for deciding if the idiom was used figuratively.

If the idiom is used in its literal meaning within the sentence, then select literal.

If it can be interpreted either way (either figuratively or literally), then select ambiguous.

If the usage of the idiom does not fall under any of the categories defined above, then select discard. The specific reasons you may select discard are the following:
  1. The idiom is not found within the sentence.
  2. The sentence is too unnatural or ungrammatical for one to comprehend.
  3. The sentence makes excessively many adjustments to the idiom that the idiom no longer holds its idiomatic interpretation.

    For example, suppose the given idiom is "burn the bridge" (which means ending a relationship) and sentence is "I ignited the bridge". Although "burn" and "ignite" are synonyms, the sentence must be discarded because the phrase "ignite the bridge" is scarcely used as an idiom.

  4. The sentence is inherently offensive.

NOTE
Each sentence may or may not have a different answer from one another (i.e., it is possible that all three of the provided sentences have the exact same answer).
